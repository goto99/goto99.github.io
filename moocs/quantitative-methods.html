Week 1 Origins of Scientific Method

Scientific method

  • empirically testable, by observations.
  • replicable
  • objective, under clear assumptions, concepts and procedures
  • transparent
  • falsifiable, or contradiction made possible.
  • logical consistent, hypothesis internally coherent, and conclusion logically consistent.

Scientific Claims

Observation
accurate/inaccurate, but doesn't describe/explain general relation
Hypothesis/Law
describe/explains pattern, general relation, hypothesis is not strongly supported, while
law is strongly supported.
Theory
overarching explanation of many related phenomena, most well established statements in science,
in science, there is no certainty.

origins of Scientific method

Plato
Rationalist, knowlege comes from reasoning.
Aristotle
Empiricist, knowlege comes through observation.
Syllogism
true premises, lead to true conclusions.
Francis Bacon
Induction (from observations of particular object to general rules) and deductions (from general
rules to predict the properties of particular objects).
Galileo Galilei
the father of modern science, separated science from philosophy, ethics and theology
